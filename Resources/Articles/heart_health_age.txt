Heart health is a lifelong journey, but the habits you build at each stage of life have different impacts. The good news is that it is never too early or too late to take positive action.

In your 20s and 30s:

This is when the foundations are built. Atherosclerosis — the gradual build-up of fatty plaques in artery walls — can begin in your 20s, though it typically takes decades to cause problems. Establishing healthy habits now creates an enormous long-term advantage. Key priorities are to not smoke, stay active, eat well, maintain a healthy weight, and manage stress. Have your blood pressure and cholesterol checked at least once during this period if you have not already.

In your 40s:

Risk factors begin to accumulate and the effects of lifestyle become more visible in health metrics. Blood pressure tends to rise with age, and cholesterol patterns often shift. Key priorities include regular monitoring of blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar. Increase the intensity and consistency of cardiovascular exercise. Pay attention to your waistline — abdominal fat is a stronger cardiovascular risk factor than overall body weight.

In your 50s and beyond:

Cardiovascular risk increases significantly with age, and for women, the menopause transition can bring marked changes in cardiovascular risk as oestrogen levels fall. Key priorities include working closely with your GP on regular screening, continuing or increasing physical activity — it is never too late for exercise to improve heart health — and reviewing any medications to ensure existing conditions are well managed.

At every age, know your numbers: blood pressure, cholesterol, blood sugar, and BMI. Do not smoke. Move your body consistently. Manage stress and prioritise sleep.

The best time to start caring for your heart was 20 years ago. The second-best time is today.
