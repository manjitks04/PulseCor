Blood pressure is the force your blood exerts against the walls of your arteries as your heart pumps. It is measured in two numbers: systolic — the pressure when your heart beats — over diastolic — the pressure when it rests between beats.

A healthy blood pressure is generally around 120/80 mmHg. High blood pressure, known as hypertension, is usually defined as consistently above 140/90 mmHg. Readings between 120–139 / 80–89 are considered elevated and worth monitoring.

High blood pressure often has no obvious symptoms, which is why it is sometimes called the silent killer. Over time, it damages arteries, increases strain on the heart, and significantly raises the risk of heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease.

Natural approaches to managing blood pressure:

Reduce sodium intake. Cutting back to under 6g of salt per day can lower systolic pressure by several points. Watch for hidden salt in bread, processed foods, and condiments. Increase potassium through foods like bananas, sweet potatoes, spinach, and avocado — potassium counteracts the effect of sodium on blood pressure. Move more — just 30 minutes of moderate aerobic activity on most days can reduce systolic blood pressure by 4–9 mmHg. Manage stress through regular breathing exercises, mindfulness, and adequate sleep. Limit alcohol, as drinking above recommended limits consistently raises blood pressure. Maintain a healthy weight, as excess body weight increases the workload on your heart.

Regular monitoring matters. Home blood pressure monitors are inexpensive and accurate. Measuring at the same time each day — typically morning before eating or drinking — gives you reliable data to share with your GP.

If lifestyle changes are not enough, medication is effective and often necessary. There is no benefit to avoiding it if your doctor recommends it.
