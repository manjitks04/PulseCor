Regular physical activity is one of the most powerful things you can do for your heart. People who move their bodies consistently have significantly lower rates of heart disease, stroke, and cardiovascular events than those who do not.

Why exercise is so effective for heart health:

It strengthens the heart muscle, making it more efficient at pumping blood. It lowers resting blood pressure. It reduces LDL cholesterol and increases HDL cholesterol. It improves blood sugar regulation, reducing the risk of type 2 diabetes — a major cardiovascular risk factor. It reduces chronic inflammation in blood vessels. It helps maintain a healthy weight, reducing the burden on your heart.

How much exercise do you need?

The NHS recommends adults aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week — that is 30 minutes, five days a week. Activities that count include brisk walking, cycling, swimming, dancing, and even gardening. Alternatively, 75 minutes of vigorous activity per week such as running or fast cycling provides similar benefits in less time.

Strength training is also important for heart health and should be included at least twice a week. Muscle mass plays a key role in metabolic health, and strong muscles reduce the effort your heart expends during everyday movement.

Starting from scratch:

If you have been inactive, start with 10–15 minute walks and build gradually. Any movement is better than none, and the cardiovascular benefits begin quickly — often within a few weeks of consistent activity.

The most important thing is finding something you enjoy. Consistency over months and years matters far more than intensity. A daily walk you actually take beats an intense gym session you keep putting off.
