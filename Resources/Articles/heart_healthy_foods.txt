What you eat every day shapes your cardiovascular health in a very direct way. Diet affects cholesterol levels, blood pressure, inflammation, and the health of your blood vessels — all key factors in heart disease risk.

The good news is that eating for heart health does not require a restrictive diet. It is more about a pattern of eating than any single food.

Foods that support your heart:

Oily fish such as salmon, mackerel, sardines, and trout are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which reduce triglycerides, lower inflammation, and support healthy heart rhythm. Aim for two portions per week. Leafy greens including spinach, kale, and rocket are high in nitrates, which help lower blood pressure. Whole grains like oats, brown rice, and wholegrain bread are rich in soluble fibre, which helps reduce LDL cholesterol. Berries are packed with antioxidants called flavonoids that have been linked to reduced heart disease risk. Extra-virgin olive oil, a cornerstone of the Mediterranean diet, is associated with significantly reduced cardiovascular disease risk. A small daily handful of walnuts, almonds, or pistachios is linked to lower LDL cholesterol and reduced inflammation. Lentils, beans, and chickpeas are high in soluble fibre and plant protein and consistently shown to improve cholesterol levels.

Foods to limit:

Ultra-processed foods — ready meals, packaged snacks, and fast food — are high in salt, sugar, and unhealthy fats. Trans fats, found in some processed foods, increase LDL and decrease HDL cholesterol. Excess salt raises blood pressure; aim for less than 6g per day. Excess sugar contributes to obesity, inflammation, and raised triglycerides.

No single food is a magic bullet, but consistently eating more of the beneficial foods and fewer of the harmful ones makes a meaningful difference over time.
